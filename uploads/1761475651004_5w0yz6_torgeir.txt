# Interview #2

## **Decision-making process without Tings Convert**

- The user prioritizes **practical aspects** like price, size, and availability when choosing products.
- There is emphasis on **visual representation**, preferring to see products modeled for better evaluation.
- Environmental considerations, such as the inability to discard clothes in regular waste, indirectly influence decisions.
- The user evaluates **brand reputation and durability** but doesn’t typically consider second-hand resale potential when purchasing new items.

**2. How Tings Convert affects decision-making**

- The presence of Tings Convert helps the user **justify purchases** by emphasizing resale potential, making high-priced items feel more viable.
- It introduces the idea of **circularity** in consumption, which aligns with growing environmental consciousness.
- The user notes that integrating Tings Convert into the shopping process could streamline **future resale**, making it an appealing feature.
- The feature positively influences decision-making if it is perceived as **user-friendly and accurate**.

**3. Most important aspects of Tings Convert**

- **“Gjensalgsverdi” (Resale Value in 2 years):** The user sees this as the most impactful metric, aiding in justifying high-cost purchases.
- **“Salgstid” (Time to Sell):** Viewed as a practical metric, though less critical than resale value.
- **“Materialscore”:** Considered informative but secondary compared to resale value and ease of resale.
- Showing “Estimert verdi om 2 år” (Estimated Value in 2 Years) instead of “Gjensalgbarhet” was well-received for its clarity and focus.

**4. Trust in Tings Convert**

- The user expressed **moderate trust (80%)** in the information provided, with some skepticism regarding whether factors like color and design were accounted for.
- The inclusion of brand-specific data (e.g., Brynje) lends credibility but requires transparency in methodology for full trust.

**5. Dislikes or drawbacks**

- Skepticism about the algorithm’s comprehensiveness, particularly its ability to account for **subjective factors** like aesthetic preferences (e.g., color).
- Concerns about the feature being too complex or overwhelming for users unfamiliar with second-hand markets.
- Some features, like “Materialscore,” may feel unnecessary or less relevant for decision-making compared to others.

**6. Key insights for future iterations**

- Focus on **Gjensalgsverdi** as the primary metric, with clear, straightforward language like “Estimert verdi om 2 år.”
- Build trust by providing **transparency about data sources** and how resale potential is calculated.
- Simplify the interface to avoid overwhelming users, highlighting key metrics most relevant to decision-making.
- Explore integration with **resale platforms** (e.g., Finn) to enhance the perceived value and practicality of Tings Convert.
- Address concerns about visual and subjective factors (e.g., style, color) to improve perceived accuracy and user trust.
