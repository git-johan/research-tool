# Insights from user interview with Tord

1. Initial Impression and Curiosity:
    1. The concept of “resellability score” and “future second-hand value” piqued interest due to its novelty/utility/uniqueness (”shopper mye I nettbutikker og har ikke sett dette”)
    2. The visual presentation (of “resellability score”) was appealing, and the interviewee expressed curiosity about the concept’s reliability and usefulness.
    3. Some skepticism arose about whether this feature was merely a marketing gimmick.
        1. “Og så er jeg en litt sånn nysgjerrig type, så jeg blir jo umiddelbart litt sånn nysgjerrig på “hva betyr det?” “Kan jeg stole på dette?” “Har det noen verdi for meg?, eller burde jeg bare avfeid det som noe markedsføring som ikke jeg trenger å bry meg om?”
        2. Therefore he wanted to dig deeper to learn, by pressing “See future value”
2. Feedback on the Chat-Based UX:
    1. The chat interface was not well-received. The interviewee found it reminiscent of generic chatbots/GPT, which they often view as unreliable or filled with irrelevant information
    2. A preference was expressed for a more structured and visually intuitive presentation, akin to platforms like Prisjakt, which use graphs and clear, data-driven layouts.
3. Importance of Data Transparency and Specificity:
    1. The interviewee appreciated the explanation of how the future value is calculated (e.g., based on historical trends, current market prices, and inflation adjustments).
    2. They noted a lack of clarity regarding the time frame of the estimated value (e.g., “70% of the price” in how many years?).
    3. Questions were raised about the granularity of data, such as accounting for variations in product condition and usage.
        1. “Det er jo interessant. Jeg spør meg hvor grundige data har dere? Og så er det så mange forskjellige variasjoner på det man kan finne brukt. Noe kan jo ha skrammer, og noe kan være ubrukt. Det er store variasjoner på der. Altså her så får ikke jeg informasjon om at estimatet gjelder om jeg bruker jakken en gang i måneden og så henger i skapet egentlig i ni måneder av året, eller om jeg bruker den daglig.”
        2. He appreciated the explanation that we base our estimates on the fact that the prices are based on USED products. Not condition “NEW”.
4. Potential Impact on Buying Decisions:
    1. The interviewee found the feature appealing and potentially valuable for deciding on high-quality, durable purchases, particularly in the second-hand market.
    2. Knowing the potential resale value might encourage investing in higher-quality products.
        1. “Jeg er også veldig opptatt av at jeg kjøper ting er av god kvalitet. Jeg har gjort for mange kjøp som viser seg å være noe dårlig og som blir ødelagt eller funker ikke tilfredsstillende. […] Hvis jeg kan oppgradere på et senere tidspunkt så er jeg kanskje interessert i det, og da tenker jeg da at da er det en fordel å vite hva eventuelt kan selge for igjen da.”
5. Skepticism About Objectivity:
    1. The claim that the assistant was “objective” was met with doubt, as the interviewee questioned the validity of such assertions from automated tools
6. Overall Perception of the Concept:
    1. The interviewee believed the feature could be a valuable addition to Norwegian e-commerce platforms, potentially setting a new standard.
    2. They emphasized the importance of providing clear, accessible information without requiring users to engage in additional steps (e.g., typing “tell me more”).
